THINGS TO AVOID WHEN OBSERVING THE JORO SPIDER!

HOME FACTS WARNINGS

DO NOT TOUCH THEM

While the spiders may not be poisonous, they can still bite you which can be very painful!

Only trained professionals should handle these creatures

Symptoms can range from minor to very severe so precautions should be taken when approaching not only these spiders, but also spiders in general.

SEVERE SYMPTOMS

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fever
  • High blood pressure

Severe symptoms for this family of spiders is very rare. These symptoms usually arise in those who are allergic to spider bites, or have a preexisting complication that may have been affected by the bite. However, even if you may not be allergic to spider bites, treat every bite with urgency and care.

MINOR SYMPTOMS

  • Itching and/or rash
  • Pain/irritation around the bite area
  • Muscle aches
  • Formation of blisters

Minor symptoms are more common, and can be treated within a week or two. Spider bites take longer to heal compared to other incest bites/stings, so don’t worry if the bite doesn’t go away for a few days. However, keep track of your symptoms, because some people may experience delayed reactions/complications.

HOW TO TREAT THE BITE

  1. Identify the bite area
  2. Clean the area thoroughly with soap and water and apply antibiotic ointment
  3. Apply a cold damp cloth over the bite area to reduce swelling and discomfort
  4. If the bite is on an extremity, such as your arm or leg, elevate it to allow better blood flow
  5. If the pain is getting a bit uncomfortable, taking over the counter pain relievers as instructed is perfectly fine
  6. Keep an eye on the bite for a few days and keep track of your symptoms
  7. Keep the area clean and repeat treatment when needed

If the bite area seems to be getting worse, or is causing extreme pain, contact a doctor immediately and recieve professional medical attention!
